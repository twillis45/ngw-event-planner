# Spades (Black-American card tradition)

## What it is / the date(s) & history
The kitchen-table card game at the center of Black American social life: partners, bidding,
relentless loving trash talk, and a strict code. Originated in **Cincinnati in the 1930s**
(commonly documented but folk-historical — treat as widely-repeated, not primary-sourced);
**popularized by Black soldiers in WWII**, spreading nationally on their return; **Pullman
porters** carried its table language ("going all the way," "running a Boston") along the rails.
Learned informally — kitchen table, family reunion, dorm, rec center — never from a manual.
*[provenance: The Root; The Pudding; BoardGameGeek; NewsOne]*

## The symbols — symbol · meaning · provenance
- **The kitchen/dining table** · the game's required home, even when played outside ·
  [The Pudding; NewsOne]
- **Partners across the table** · trust and silent communication; "how you play Spades is how you
  play life" · [The Pudding]
- **Trash talk** · part of the game's DNA — loving, sharp, reads as affection to insiders ·
  [NewsOne; The Pudding]
- **The code (never renege)** · reneging = failing to follow suit when able = cheating, penalized;
  a "Boston" = winning all 13 books; "set" = missing your bid · [The Game of Spades glossary;
  The Root]

## Colors & flag — hex + what each color means (identity only)
- **Table Steel `#5E6E8C`** — muted slate-blue, the felt-and-evening tone of a long table game;
  calm, communal, heritage. IDENTITY ONLY. *Blue family — no overlap with red/green/amber status
  hues.*
- (The spade pip itself is near-black; the slate frames it.)

## Food & drink — dish/drink · symbolism
Cookout-adjacent — the game runs alongside a backyard cookout/family-reunion spread; food and
play share the evening. (No single canonical dish; it travels with whatever's being grilled.)

## Music & ritual — the gathering, the cues, who's honored
Generational — taught down the family; elders' table authority; the bid called around the table;
the long Friday-night session. A "safe space to gather, connect, and be Black." · [The Pudding;
NewsOne]

## Heart-moments — the moments that must happen (feeds playbooks)
- The deck cut and partners set.
- The bid called — and the threat of a Boston.
- The code held — nobody reneges.

## Regional / diaspora notes
A defining thread of shared Black American culture nationwide; in the DMV it sits inside the
cookout/family-reunion/go-go social world. Reverence required — heritage, not "game night."

## In the product
- **Icon:** a single spade pip drawn as TWO interlocking strokes (partners), steel line; Table-
  Steel accent on the seam.
- **Identity color:** Table Steel `#5E6E8C` — IDENTITY only, never a UI status hue.
- **Copy do/don't:** DO — "never renege," "set the bid," "the code," partners, the kitchen table,
  learned-from-someone. DON'T — Vegas/casino/gambling framing, jokey "card night" clip-art,
  anything that flattens it into a generic party game.
