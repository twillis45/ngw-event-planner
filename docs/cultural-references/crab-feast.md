# Crab Feast (Maryland / Chesapeake)

## What it is / the date(s) & history
The Maryland blue-crab feast: friends and family gathered around a long, brown-paper-covered
table to steam, season, crack, and pick **Chesapeake blue crabs** all afternoon. A summer
ritual (peak season roughly **April–early winter**, best mid-summer through Labor Day). Native
peoples — the **Piscataway and Powhatan** — harvested Chesapeake blue crabs **~3,200 years
ago**; European colonists continued it from the 1600s; commercial crabbing expanded after the
crab pot's invention (late 19th–early 20th c.). The blue crab became Maryland's **official state
crustacean in 1989**. The **National Hard Crab Derby** (Crisfield, since 1947–48, Labor Day
weekend) is the tradition's festival form. *[provenance: Cameron's Seafood; Sweet Tea + Thyme;
Southern Maryland Chronicle; National Hard Crab Derby]*

## The symbols — symbol · meaning · provenance
- **Brown paper / newspaper on the table** · the feast is hands-on, messy, communal — the table
  *is* the event · [Cameron's Seafood; Sweet Tea + Thyme]
- **Mallet & knife** · the slow, deliberate labor of picking a crab; the pace forces conversation ·
  [Cameron's Seafood]
- **Old Bay (or JO Spice)** · regional identity in seasoning; "stains your fingers orange" ·
  [Sweet Tea + Thyme; Maryland Recommendations]
- **Steaming, not boiling** · Maryland orthodoxy — boiling is held to make the meat soggy ·
  [Sweet Tea + Thyme]

## Colors & flag — hex + what each color means (identity only)
- **Steamed Shell `#C46A3A`** — the burnt-orange a blue crab turns under steam + seasoning;
  warmth, summer, the Bay. IDENTITY ONLY. *Kept distinct from warning-amber and danger-red
  `#C03838`.*
- (Live crab's blue-green is descriptive, not the identity accent — the cooked shell is the
  emotional color.)

## Food & drink — dish/drink · symbolism
- **Steamed blue crabs, heavy Old Bay** · the centerpiece; the labor is the love.
- **Sides:** corn, sometimes shrimp/sausage steam.
- **National Bohemian ("Natty Boh")** · the DMV's light lager, the canonical crab drink ·
  [Maryland Recommendations]

## Music & ritual — the gathering, the cues, who's honored
The afternoon-long pace; elders and out-of-towners served the first/best crabs; the steam pot as
the gathering's clock; backyard/waterfront setting; cold beer.

## Heart-moments — the moments that must happen (feeds playbooks)
- The table covered and the first steaming dozen dumped out.
- Hands going in together — no one eating fast.
- Four hours later, still at the table.

## Regional / diaspora notes
Pure DMV/Chesapeake. Steaming (not boiling) is the regional dividing line vs. Louisiana/Gulf
boils. Natty Boh as the regional beverage. Crisfield as "Crab Capital of the World."
