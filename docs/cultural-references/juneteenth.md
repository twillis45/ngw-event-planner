# Juneteenth

## What it is / history
**June 19, 1865** — Union General Gordon Granger arrived in Galveston, Texas and issued **General Order No. 3**, announcing freedom to the ~250,000 still-enslaved Black Americans there — **2.5 years after** the Emancipation Proclamation. "Juneteenth" (June + nineteenth) is the oldest nationally celebrated commemoration of the end of slavery in the U.S.; it became a U.S. federal holiday in 2021.

## The flag & its symbolism  (the design to ground our glyph in)
Designed by activist **Ben Haith** (founder of the National Juneteenth Celebration Foundation) in **1997**; brought to final form by illustrator **Lisa Jeanne Graf**. The date "June 19, 1865" was added **in 2000**. Every element carries meaning:

- **The star (center):** Represents **Texas** — the Lone Star State, where the last enslaved people learned of their freedom — AND the freedom of African Americans across **all 50 states**.
- **The bursting outline / "nova" around the star:** A **nova** — a new star, a burst — symbolizing a **new beginning / a new freedom / a new people** for Black Americans.
- **The arc (the curve across the middle):** A **new horizon** — the opportunities and promise that lay ahead.
- **The colors — red, white, blue:** Deliberately the **same as the American flag** — signifying that the enslaved and their descendants **were and are Americans**, and Juneteenth is an American legacy.

## The flag's four marks (verbatim symbolism, from the canonical explainer — `review-artifacts/Juneteenth-Flag (1).jpg`)
- **THE STAR** — the white 5-point star at center represents both **Texas** (the Lone Star State) and the **freedom of African Americans in all 50 states**.
- **THE BURST** — the **bursting outline around the star** is inspired by a **nova**, the astronomers' term for a new star: **a new beginning for African Americans**.
- **THE ARC** — the **curve that extends across the width of the flag** represents a **new horizon**: the opportunities and promise that lay ahead for Black Americans.
- **THE COLORS** — **red, white, and blue** mirror the American flag, a reminder that **the enslaved and their descendants were and are Americans**.
- **THE DATE** — *June 19, 1865* — the day enslaved African Americans in Galveston, Texas learned of their freedom.

## Glyph construction (match the flag — `review-artifacts/Juneteenth-Flag (1).jpg`)
The Juneteenth identity glyph = three nested elements as a clean **line** mark:
1. A **5-point star** at center (point up).
2. A **12-point bursting star OUTLINE** that **hugs/surrounds** the 5-point star — a single continuous 12-pointed star shape (the "nova"), the central star nested inside it. **NOT** detached radiating rays/sparkle lines, and **NOT** a flower/cog — it is one 12-point burst-star outline around the star, exactly as on the flag.
3. A **horizon arc** — a shallow curve **spanning the FULL WIDTH** that passes **through the middle of the star/burst** (the emblem sits ON the horizon, the boundary between the blue field above and red below). Not an underline beneath the star.
**Line weight = FINE / hairline** — refined and sophisticated, not heavy. Cultural-red identity line (identity, not status); scalable from ~16px header to hero. *(Flag: Ben Haith / NJCF, finalized by Lisa Jeanne Graf; date added 2000.)*

## Food & drink — symbolism
**Red** foods/drinks dominate — red signifies **resilience, the blood and sacrifice of the enslaved, and West African heritage** (hibiscus/sorrel, kola). Common: **red drink** (hibiscus/sorrel, strawberry, red soda), **red velvet cake**, **watermelon**, **red beans**; the **cookout/BBQ** (smoked meats) as the gathering form.

## Music & ritual
Spirituals, blues, and the cookout's music; **reading General Order No. 3** / acknowledgment of the day; **libation** (pouring for ancestors); **elders honored/served first**.

## Regional / diaspora (DMV)
Half-smokes + **mumbo sauce** (Capital City), **go-go** (Chuck Brown / EU) — the DMV Juneteenth cookout sound.
